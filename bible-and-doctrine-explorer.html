The Old Testament

The Old Testament is the first division of the Christian biblical canon (standardized collection of texts). It contains books of law, history, poetry, and prophecy that form the foundation of the biblical narrative, chronicling God's covenant relationship with His people.

Book distribution by division in the Old Testament.

The New Testament

The New Testament is the second division of the Christian biblical canon. It discusses the teachings and person of Jesus, as well as events in first-century Christianity. Its sections include the Gospels, the history of the early church, various letters (epistles), and apocalyptic prophecy.

Book distribution by division in the New Testament.

The Apocrypha

The Apocrypha, sometimes called the 'Deuterocanonical' (meaning 'second canon') books, are books or passages that are considered by some Christian denominations to be canonical, but are not accepted by others (such as most Protestant denominations). They often provide historical context, wisdom literature, or prophetic visions from the intertestamental period.
